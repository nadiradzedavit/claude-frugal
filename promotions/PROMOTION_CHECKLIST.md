## Emergency Responses

### If You Get Negative Feedback
1. Thank them for taking time to comment
2. Acknowledge valid points
3. Explain reasoning without being defensive
4. If they're right, commit to fixing it
5. Follow up when fixed

**Example:**
> "You're absolutely right that the 97% claim needs more validation. That's why I'm transparently stating it's pre-validation phase and actively seeking beta testers. I'll make this clearer in the README. Thanks for keeping me honest!"

### If Someone Calls It Spam
1. Apologize immediately
2. Explain it's a genuine OSS project
3. Don't argue
4. Remove/edit if appropriate
5. Learn for next time

### If Metrics Are Lower Than Expected
1. Don't panic - most launches don't go viral
2. Review what worked vs. what didn't
3. Adjust messaging
4. Keep building
5. Remember: slow and steady wins

### If You Get Overwhelmed
1. It's okay to take breaks
2. Set boundaries (e.g., "I'll respond within 24 hours")
3. Focus on quality over speed
4. Ask for help in Discussions
5. Remember why you built this
